Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)

Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR) is a technique developed by American physician Edmund Jacobson in the 1920s. It involves tensing and then relaxing each muscle group in the body to reduce muscle tension and promote relaxation.

What is Progressive Muscle Relaxation?

PMR is a form of therapy that helps release tension from muscles while helping individuals recognize and manage stress.

Benefits of PMR

Reduces anxiety and tension

Improves sleep quality

Eases neck and low back pain

Decreases systolic blood pressure

Reduces the frequency of migraine attacks

Helps with temporomandibular joint (TMJ) symptoms

How to Do Progressive Muscle Relaxation

PMR involves tensing and then relaxing each muscle group in the body. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by lying or sitting down in a quiet, comfortable area.
  2. Take five deep, slow breaths to relax.
  3. Focus on one muscle group at a time, tensing it for a few seconds, then relaxing it for twice as long before moving on to the next muscle group.
  4. Tense your toes by curling them downward. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  5. Tense your calf muscles by pointing your toes upward. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  6. Tense your thigh muscles by pressing your knees together. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  7. Contract your buttocks by squeezing them tightly. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  8. Make fists with your hands and clench your fingers. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  9. Tighten your biceps by flexing your arms. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  10. Bring your shoulders up towards your ears to tense your shoulder muscles. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  11. Press your tongue against the roof of your mouth to tense your jaw muscles. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  12. Close your eyes tightly to scrunch up your facial muscles. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax for 10 seconds.
  13. Repeat the entire process if desired, focusing on deeper relaxation with each repetition.

Tips for Beginners

  1. Set aside 15 to 20 minutes for PMR in a quiet, comfortable area.
  2. Turn off your phone to avoid distractions.
  3. Avoid holding your breath; inhale deeply when tensing muscles and exhale fully when relaxing.
  4. Move in a sequence that works for you.
  5. Wear loose, lightweight clothing.
  6. Practice PMR regularly, even when feeling calm, to make it easier to learn.
  7. Consider listening to a PMR recording for guidance.
